A peak into history

Ribat, Monastir 16 Sep. 2024

The Ribat of Monastir, located on the central coast of Tunisia, is an impressive Islamic fortress built in the 8th century. It served as both a military stronghold and an important religious center during the Aghlabid dynasty. The Ribat is known for its robust architecture and simplicity, blending defensive and spiritual elements. Positioned strategically by the sea, it was also used to protect the coast from external invasions. Today, the Ribat of Monastir is a major tourist attraction and a symbol of the region's Islamic heritage. It also offers stunning views of the city of Monastir and the Mediterranean Sea.

Mediterranean Blue

Sidi Bou Said, 14 Sep. 2024

Sidi Bou Said is a picturesque coastal village in Tunisia, famous for its stunning white-and-blue architecture, which reflects the vibrant colors of the Mediterranean. Perched on a hill overlooking the sea, the village is a serene blend of history, art, and nature. The iconic blue doors, shutters, and wrought-iron balconies create a striking contrast against the whitewashed buildings, making Sidi Bou Said one of the most photographed spots in Tunisia. With its charming narrow streets, beautiful views, and artistic atmosphere, it has long been a favorite retreat for artists and travelers alike.
